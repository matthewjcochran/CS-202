Model: Exponential Growth¶

Differential equation¶

$$ \frac{d y}{d t} = k y $$

Define terms.

term meaning (covid-19)
$ y $ number (cases)
$ t $ time (day number)
$ \frac{d y}{d t} $ rate of change (new cases per day)
$ k $ constant (percent increase per day $\approx 0.25$)

The differential equation says that the case increase per day is proportional to the number of cases.

Numerical solutions¶

Rewrite the differential equation.

$$ \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta t} = k y \qquad \rightarrow \qquad \Delta y = k y \Delta t $$

Make a table stepping by day $\Delta t = 1$. Assume that percent increase per day is $k = 0.25$ and that we start with $C = 64$ cases.

$t$ $y$ $\Delta y$
0 64 16
1 80 20
2 100 25
3 125 ...
... ... ...

Analytic solutions¶

Need Calculus for this.

$$ y = C e^{k t} $$$$ \frac{d y}{d t} = C k e^{k t} $$

For our example.

$$ y = 64 e^{0.25 t} $$$$ \frac{d y}{d t} = 16 e^{0.25 t} $$

Model: Logistic Growth¶

Differential equation¶

$$ \frac{d y}{d t} = k \bigg(1 - \frac{y}{L} \bigg) y $$

Define terms.

term meaning (covid-19)
$ y $ number (cases)
$ t $ time (day number)
$ \frac{d y}{d t} $ rate of change (new cases per day)
$ k $ constant (percent increase per day $\approx 0.25$)
$ L $ carrying capacity (maximum number of cases $\approx ???$)

When $y << L$, the differential equation says that the case increase per day is proportional to the number of cases. When $y = L$, growth stops.

Analytic solutions¶

Need Calculus for this.

$$ y = \frac{L}{1 + b e^{-k x}} $$$$ \frac{d y}{d t} = \frac{L b k e^{-k t}}{(1 + b e^{-k t})^2} $$$$ \mathrm{where} \quad b = \frac{L}{C} - 1 $$

L = 100_000 # carrying capacity is 100,000 in Hawaii???
b = L/C - 1
